The Ketogenic Diet is a medical therapy, most often used to treat patients with Medication Resistant Epilepsy. When used to treat epilepsy, the KGD is closely monitored by dietitians, physicians, and the healthcare team. When using the KGD as an experimental treatment for cancer, it should also be closely monitored, not undertaken by individuals without medical experience or access to routine lab work and necessary prescription supplements.

Denise Potter RD, CSP, CDE

Denise has been a Registered Dietitian (RD) for 27 years. She has been administering the Ketogenic Diet to children with epilepsy and other conditions at the University of Michigan CS Mott Children's Hospital for 9 years. Prior to her interest in the Ketogenic Diet, she specialized in Diabetes Education with Promedia Health System.

She has been a CDE (Certified Diabetes Educator) for 19 years and a CSP (Certified Specialist in Pediatrics) for 3 years.

Denise received her BS in Dietetics from Michigan State University and completed her Dietetic Internship at the University of Arkansas for Medical Sciences.
